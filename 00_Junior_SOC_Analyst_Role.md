# Junior SOC Analyst Role Overview

## Role Details

- **Role:** Junior SOC Analyst
- **Typical Team:** Security Operations Center (SOC)
- **Work Environment:** Internal security team, managed security provider, or hybrid IT/security operations team

---

## Role Overview

A Junior SOC Analyst supports day-to-day security monitoring, alert triage, and incident handling. The role usually involves reviewing alerts from SIEM, endpoint, email, identity, and network tools, then documenting findings and escalating issues when needed.

This guide is written as a public learning resource for people preparing for entry-level SOC work or strengthening their practical security operations skills.

---

## Role Summary

A Junior SOC Analyst helps detect, validate, document, and escalate potential security incidents using structured procedures.

---

## Responsibilities

- Monitor alerts from SIEM and endpoint tools
- Perform initial triage and validation
- Escalate incidents based on procedures
- Analyze phishing attempts
- Document investigations
- Follow runbooks and playbooks
- Participate in training and exercises

---

## Common Requirements

- Foundational cybersecurity knowledge
- Familiarity with logs and alerts
- Strong attention to detail
- Ability to follow structured procedures
- Clear written communication
- Willingness to learn from feedback and documented processes

---

## Helpful Background

- Entry-level IT or cybersecurity experience
- Exposure to SIEM, EDR, email security, or identity tools
- Understanding of basic networking and operating system concepts
- Interest in incident response, threat detection, and security operations

---

## How to Use This Guide

Use the numbered files as a study path. Start with monitoring and triage, then move into escalation, phishing analysis, documentation, runbooks, and training exercises. The companion example files are designed to help connect concepts to realistic SOC situations.
