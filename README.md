# Junior SOC Analyst - Complete Responsibility Guide

## 📌 Overview

This comprehensive guide breaks down common Junior SOC Analyst responsibilities into detailed review points, step-by-step guides, references, practice activities, and real-world examples.

---

## 🎯 Responsibilities Overview

### 1. [Monitor Alerts from SIEM and Endpoint Tools](01_0_SIEM_Monitoring_Endpoint_Alerts.md)
Continuously monitor security information and event management (SIEM) systems and endpoint detection and response (EDR) tools for security alerts.

**Key Topics:**
- Dashboard navigation and alert triage
- Alert severity classifications
- Alert correlation and pattern recognition
- Metrics and KPI tracking

**Time to Master:** 2-4 weeks

**Advanced Resource:** [SIEM Monitoring: Attack-Specific Queries & Indicators](01_1_SIEM_Monitoring_Attack_Specific_Queries.md) - Practical guide with syntaxes, queries, and event IDs for different attack types (Windows & Linux)

**Practice Resource:** [SIEM Monitoring Real-World Examples](01_2_SIEM_Monitoring_Real_World_Examples.md) - Realistic monitoring scenarios for MFA fatigue, suspicious PowerShell, and VPN spraying

---

### 2. [Perform Initial Triage and Validation](02_0_Triage_Validation.md)
Assess incoming alerts to determine legitimacy, gather context, and decide on appropriate next actions.

**Key Topics:**
- Triage frameworks and methodologies
- Context gathering and analysis
- False positive identification
- Risk assessment basics

**Time to Master:** 4-6 weeks

**Practice Resource:** [Triage Validation Real-World Examples](02_1_Triage_Validation_Examples.md) - Scenario-based triage examples that follow the validation workflow

---

### 3. [Escalate Incidents Based on Procedures](03_0_Incident_Escalation_Procedures.md)
Follow established escalation procedures to route incidents to appropriate teams based on severity, type, and organizational criteria.

**Key Topics:**
- Escalation criteria and procedures
- Severity classification
- SLA management
- Communication protocols

**Time to Master:** 2-3 weeks

**Practice Resource:** [Incident Escalation Real-World Examples](03_1_Incident_Escalation_Real_World_Examples.md) - Realistic escalation examples with priority, contacts, and handoff messages

---

### 4. [Analyze Phishing Attempts](04_0_Phishing_Analysis.md)
Investigate suspected phishing emails to identify threats, analyze indicators, and determine appropriate actions.

**Key Topics:**
- Email header analysis
- Link and attachment analysis
- Social engineering tactics
- Threat intelligence research

**Time to Master:** 4-6 weeks

**Advanced Resource:** [Phishing Email Analysis: Comprehensive Tutorial](04_1_Phishing_Email_Analysis_Tutorial.md) - Complete breakdown of email structure and detailed analysis guidance

**Practice Resource:** [Phishing Practice Emails](04_2_Phishing_Practice_Emails.md) - Six practice emails with analysis questions and full explanations

---

### 5. [Document Investigations](05_0_Investigation_Documentation.md)
Maintain comprehensive, accurate records of all security investigations and incident responses.

**Key Topics:**
- Incident documentation standards
- Timeline creation and management
- Evidence preservation
- Report writing

**Time to Master:** 3-4 weeks

**Practice Resource:** [Investigation Documentation Real-World Examples](05_1_Investigation_Documentation_Real_World_Examples.md) - Example summaries, timelines, evidence notes, and closing comments

---

### 6. [Follow Runbooks and Playbooks](06_0_Runbooks_Playbooks.md)
Execute established incident response procedures consistently and accurately.

**Key Topics:**
- Runbook vs. playbook distinction
- Procedure navigation and execution
- Decision point management
- Procedure improvement requests

**Time to Master:** 2-4 weeks

**Practice Resource:** [Runbooks and Playbooks Real-World Examples](06_1_Runbooks_Playbooks_Real_World_Examples.md) - Practical examples for following phishing, lost laptop, and ransomware procedures

---

### 7. [Participate in Training and Exercises](07_0_Training_Exercises.md)
Actively engage in security training, exercises, and drills to build and maintain skills.

**Key Topics:**
- Training types and formats
- Tabletop exercises and simulations
- Phishing awareness drills
- Certification and professional development

**Time to Master:** Ongoing (6-12 months)

**Practice Resource:** [Training and Exercises Real-World Examples](07_1_Training_Exercises_Real_World_Examples.md) - Tabletop, phishing drill, and shadowing examples for SOC learning

---

## 📚 Learning Roadmap

### Month 1-2: Foundation Building
- Complete [Monitor Alerts from SIEM and Endpoint Tools](01_0_SIEM_Monitoring_Endpoint_Alerts.md)
- Complete [Follow Runbooks and Playbooks](06_0_Runbooks_Playbooks.md)
- Begin [Perform Initial Triage and Validation](02_0_Triage_Validation.md)
- **Certification Goal:** Assess current knowledge

### Month 2-3: Core Skills Development
- Master [Perform Initial Triage and Validation](02_0_Triage_Validation.md)
- Master [Escalate Incidents Based on Procedures](03_0_Incident_Escalation_Procedures.md)
- Complete [Analyze Phishing Attempts](04_0_Phishing_Analysis.md)
- **Certification Goal:** CompTIA Security+ preparation

### Month 3-4: Advanced Skills
- Master [Analyze Phishing Attempts](04_0_Phishing_Analysis.md)
- Master [Document Investigations](05_0_Investigation_Documentation.md)
- Begin specialized training in your organization's tools
- **Certification Goal:** Continue Security+ prep

### Month 4-6: Specialization & Mastery
- Achieve mastery across all core responsibilities
- Develop expertise in specific incident types
- Participate actively in training and exercises
- **Certification Goal:** Pass Security+ exam or equivalent

### Month 6-12: Advanced Development & Mentoring
- Develop expertise in specialized areas
- Begin mentoring newer team members
- Work toward advanced certifications (GCIH, etc.)
- Contribute to procedure improvements
